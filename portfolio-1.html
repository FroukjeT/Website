Programming

Within the study Creative Technology, it is stimulated to develop yourself outside of the regular courses by choosing your own extracurricular activities. Several workshops are provided via the course professional development, as well as guest speakers helping you to define your profile as Creative Technologist.

In this workshop, an introduction to the programming language javaScript has been given. Basic syntax and semantic structures are given, after which we got the task to create our own javascript game. The game I created is called ping-pong, which is bouncing a ball from the left to the right without missing it with your stick. It is a multiplayer game, and if one of the players misses the ball, there will be a message that the player has lost. Highscores are kept in the system and shown to the players.

Introduction to Processing

Programming

In the first module of Creative technology, an introduction to programming is given via the course ‘programming’. In this course, the programming language processing (relatable to java) is being taught. During the course, students are encouraged to design their own creature, and every week update this creature either in components or in programming style. This starts with making ellipses and rectangles, goes on to movement and interaction, and ends with making coherent methods, classes, and other style elements.

The creature I created was a pig, which was floating through space. The background is made up of a sun, moving clouds, and moving stars. There is a function sleep, which will activate when you press a key. When the function sleep activates, the pig’s eyes will close and his ears will rotate out. The sun will also change into a moon. You can also drag the pigs over the screen. When you drag the pig over the screen, the belly will change towards the position you are going to. Visual Communication

Design

In the first module of Creative Technology, a course called ‘Visual Communication’ is given. In this course, students are taught how to visually present information/objects to an audience. The final goal of the course is to create a poster, which is being achieved through several stages.

To start, multiple headers/pieces of text from newspapers are collected. These headings are cut up and used to create a new story (see first picture). After having created this story, feelings are connected to it. Students are told to create a face that comes with this feeling, as long as they were not real faces of people. The faces that I have created are made out of newspapers, small book covers and big book covers. The result can be seen in the pictures below. Finally, the faces were connected to a company with a logo and a slogan for a campaign.

By following this method for creating posters, ideas have come up that otherwise could not have been achieved. It is a way of creative thinking. In the pictures below, one face and two designs for posters can be seen.

Interactive video

Module project

Every module of Creative Technology has a module project, in which students work together in a team to bring together the knowledge of various courses of that module into one project. Since this is the first module of the program, the module project was mainly focussed on working together and creating content. The goal of this module project was to create an interactive video.Students had to make a video that had several storylines, so users could choose their own path and generate their own movie.

Three friends are brought back together by the unknown to save their kidnapped friend. They have to complete four challenges to save their friend. Who would be behind all this… Can you make the right choices to save your friend?

This project is made together with Duncan Bosman, Dennis Peeters, Mark Ziegelhofer and Jordy van Hierden. The filming is done around Enschede and a part in Gronau, Germany. The poster and trailer of the movie can be found above and below.
