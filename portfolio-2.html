For the module project in the second module, a vertical garden is made that moves towards the sun to get optional sunlight. The vertical garden has several sensors such as soil moisture sensors, humidity sensors and temperature sensors, to be able to optimally water itself. In this project, I took care of the communication between the sensors and actuators via Arduino, as well as the GUI for the computer in processing.

Physical Computing

Programming

In the second module of creative technology, the course physical computing is given. In this course, an introduction to Arduino is given, such as several sensors and Arduino libraries. As a final project, students are encouraged to bring all sensors together in a creative solution that addresses userâ€™s needs.

In this project, I have created a smart stove together with another student. A user can choose a recipe and turn the program on. The processing interface (client based, meaning it could be run on your smartphone), shows where to put the products needed for the recipe on the plate. The stove consists of placeholders for products, red and green lights for every spot, a display, a speaker with adjustable volume button, and a touchscreen on/off button. The sensors are light sensors that detects if products are there, capacitive sensors to generate the touchscreen, and buttons to continue to next steps. Finally, a potentiometer is used to adjust the volume of the speaker.
