Biomedical Engineering

Electronics

Module 8 was all about big data, in this case the course Biomedical Engineering. In this course we learned how to take a lot of data out of an IMU, an inertial measurement unit. An IMU is a device that has a velocity part and an gyroscope part.

In our project we wanted to increase the performance of a runner by making an interactive feedback system. We placed two IMU's on a runners leg and measured how straight the knee was going with regard do it's initial position. We focussed on sprints, meaning it's most efficient if you don't move right/left with your knee but just straightforward.

We used haptic feedback on the knee of the runner to let the runner in realtime know that he should focus on putting his knee more straight.


  • Date: June 2021
  • Purpose: The course biomedical engineering
  • Category: Electronics

Module Project

Big Data

Module 8 was all about big data, in this case the module project. The goals was to make a really big installation that visualizes a big database.

Our topic was water levels in rivers in the netherlands. We made a huge physical table containing a river and houses, in which the river could change water height according to the data. Additionally, water tubes on top showed the comparison between cities and an interactive water cup system was used to select a city. To select a date, a turning wheel was used.

The main purpose was to create awareness in how our water levels are changing over time, trying to direct users to change.

  • Date: June 2021
  • Purpose: Module project
  • Category: Electronics / big data
